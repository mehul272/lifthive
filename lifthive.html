⚡ The Problem

Why gyms fail people —
not the other way around

India's gym dropout rate stays above 60%. These are the six core reasons nobody's fixed yet.

🚶
Solo Syndrome

You join alone, you quit alone. No accountability loop, no social pull, no one to push you when motivation tanks.

🗺️
Gym Blindness

Finding the right gym is guesswork. Google Maps shows a pin — not crowd levels, trainer quality, or whether it fits your vibe.

💸
Trainer Unaffordability

Good trainers cost ₹3,000–8,000/month. That's out of reach for most gym-goers on their own.

🎵
Playlist Friction

Curating the right energy for chest day vs. cardio vs. leg day is a manual chore. Music should flow with the workout.

📉
Invisible Progress

There's no easy way to see if you're actually getting stronger — not just in a vacuum, but compared to your gym peers.

🔇
No Celebration Layer

You hit a 1RM PR or a 30-day streak and nobody in your social feed cares. Fitness has no native reward system.

🎯 For Gym Goers · Phase 1

Five things that
change the game

LiftHive's core product is built entirely around the gym-goer. Every feature solves a real daily pain point.

🏆
Gym Leaderboard

Real-time rankings across your gym — weekly points for check-ins, sets completed, and PRs hit. Compete with everyone at your gym or call out specific people directly. This is the social pull that keeps you showing up when motivation alone doesn't.

Core Feature · Social Retention
⚡
Progressive Overload Challenges

Challenge a friend to a progressive overload race — same muscle group, same time window, most improvement wins. Built-in accountability with real stakes and zero guessing.

Virality · Peer Challenge
📍
GymMap

Advanced gym discovery — real member reviews, live crowd estimates, trainer ratings, equipment inventory, and vibe scores. Find your perfect gym, not just the closest one.

Discovery · Acquisition Hook
🎵
GymBeats

Playlists synced to your muscle group with automatic set rest timers. Chest day sounds different from cardio — and the next set starts when the rest ends. Zero manual curation.

Daily Utility · Stickiness
🤝
TrainTogether

Split a personal trainer's cost across 2–4 people. Book together, train together, pay a fraction. Quality coaching goes from a luxury to a shared expense.

🔍 What We Need to Crack

Big questions before
we write one line of code

30-day sprint. Real conversations. These two buckets decide if we build what we think — or something different.

💰
Monetization Unknowns
1
Will gym-goers in Pune pay ₹99–199/month for a social gym app — or does it need to be free (ads/freemium) to get any traction?
2
Which single feature makes someone actually reach for their wallet — leaderboard bragging rights, GymBeats, or access to a cheaper trainer?
3
Does the TrainTogether commission model hold, or will users and trainers negotiate directly and cut us out after the first session?
4
Is ₹999/year (one-time) more appealing than ₹149/month? Which payment format creates least resistance in this price segment?
5
Can GymMap work as the free acquisition hook — basic discovery free, premium unlocks real-time crowd data and trainer ratings?
🏢
Gym Owner Validation
1
Will gym owners allow free listing on GymMap without paying us anything? It's organic SEO for them — but will they trust a new app?
2
Do gym owners want their members competing on a public leaderboard — or does it create anxiety, comparison pressure, or member drop-off?
3
Will trainers voluntarily join TrainTogether, or do they fear being commoditized or losing the ₹3,000–8,000/month rate they currently charge?
4
Which 3 gyms in Baner, Kothrud, or Wakad will be our first partners — and what do they actually want in exchange for early access?
5
What's the current cost-per-member-acquisition for a mid-size Pune gym? This number defines our value pitch when we approach owners in Phase 3.
